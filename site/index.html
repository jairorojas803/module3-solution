Chicken

Chicken meat is what the muscle tissues and organs of chicken are called. It is very common to find it in many dishes and culinary preparations around the world. Its meat is considered a staple food and it is for this reason that it is included in the consumer price index. The origin of the relationship of this species with humans dates back to the Neolithic, and specifically within the framework of the change from hunter-gatherer societies to farmers-livestock. Some studies reveal that the first domesticated hens and chickens may have come from India, more than 4,000 years ago. His presence in the life of man is not new. The first remains date from the Neolithic, from the year 6000 a. They were found in the Chinese province of Hebei and from there they passed to Europe at the hands of the Sumerians. In the Egypt of Tuthmosis III (1500 BC) there is already evidence of poultry farmers, and in the gastronomic treatises of Rome in the 1st century AD. C. Advances in science, as well as new hygienic-sanitary knowledge, allowed the spread of chicken consumption to the entire population during the 20th century ”. Its prodigious decade arrived in 1960. It stopped being a luxury item to become a regular product in diets due to its high protein and vitamin content and the very low presence of fat.It is usually used in a wide variety of preparations ranging from grilled (barbecue), oven roasted, fried, battered fried, stewed or in the preparation of soups and broths. You can see its minced meat turned into cold cuts, hamburgers and other elaborated products (mainly the cut called breast, since by itself it has little flavor and very low fat content). Some of its components are associated with the preparation of fast food and some chains such as Kentucky Fried Chicken have specialized in serving portions of this meat on their plates.La carne de pollo es una de las más saludables del mercado. Es un alimento con una alta densidad de nutrientes. El principal componente de la carne de pollo es el agua, que representa del 70% al 75% del total; las proteínas suponen entre el 20% y el 22%; y, por último, la grasa, entre un 3% y un 10%. En su composición también figuran cantidades importantes de minerales como hierro, zinc, magnesio, selenio, cobalto, fósforo, cromo, vitamina A, B1, B3, B6, B12 y rivoflavina La cantidad de grasa del pollo varía según la parte que se consume. En las piezas más magras, el porcentaje es bajo. La mayor parte está en la piel, con casi 48 gramos de grasa por cada 100 gramos de carne. La grasa es un aspecto que depende directamente de la alimentación del animal durante su crecimiento.The brood chicken is slaughtered at a minimum age of 20 weeks and weighing between one and three kilos. Among the different types of chicken meat and, depending on sex and age at the time of slaughter, several stand out. Free-range chicken (the term "free-range chicken" is sometimes used, but is not regulated1). It is the smallest of all, its meat is the tastiest, as long as it has been fed in a varied way, but also a little tougher. Spicy chicken. It is sacrificed when it is one month old and its weight can reach 500 grams. It is an appropriate meat to prepare on the grill, tender and with a mild flavor. Farm chicken. It is the most common and the cheapest. This bird feeds only on feed, grows in a very short time and also moves very little, given the characteristics of the space where it lives. Pularda. This young castrated female who has not laid and is overfed can reach up to three kilos in weight. She is slaughtered at six or eight months of age and her meat is tender and very tasty. Capon. He is a young and overfed gelding. It can reach four kilos in weight before slaughter. His meat has a streaky fat that gives it a specific aroma and tenderness. It is the traditional Christmas chicken that is stuffed. Broiler chicken. Of little weight, about 500 grams or a maximum kilo, this bird has been fed with fresh tomatoes. It has a special flavor, between soft and firm. Chicken. It is the adult female that is sacrificed after exhausting its egg-laying capacity, has more fat than chicken and is suitable for broths or stews.
